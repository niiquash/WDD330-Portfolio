Week 1 Reading Notes

Mobile UX Videos

  • Designing for smallest first leads to beautiful and powerful designs
  • PCs are replaced every 4-5 years. Mobile phone are replaced every 1-2 years
  • More mobile traffic online has been recorded. Hence, build mobile first.
  • Responsive is not equal to a mobile first design.
  • Start small and scale up.
  • Mobile is not a fad. We’re not getting rid of mobile devices anytime soon.

Mobile Design Strategies

  • Be there for the user. Help then through their journey
  • Be useful. Provide users with what they came for.
  • Be quick. Reduce their cognitive load. Make your interactions smooth and seamless. Example take a 5 steps task and make it 0 steps.
  • Speed matters. Reduce image usage throughout sites. If images are used, use svg/ webfonts which have smaller bandwidth. Optimize and minify CSS and JS files. GZip files to eliminate unnecessary data.

Primary Controls

  • Placement matters. Address reachability issues. It creates a perception of speed.
  • Understanding matters. Example the hamburger menu icon is confusing to most who don’t understand the function of the icon.

Intuitive and Mobile Friendly Forms

  • Ditch form labels and have them display inside the field instead.
  • Float labels makes it a whole lot easier for users to fill out forms.
  • Enable autocomplete on forms.
  • Show password first then give users the option to hide passwords.
  • When asking for numbers, ensure the number pad is displayed on mobile
  • Make it easy for users to call your number with a single tap.
